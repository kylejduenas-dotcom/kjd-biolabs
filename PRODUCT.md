# KJD BioLabs — Product Context

register: brand

## Product purpose

A direct-to-researcher e-commerce store for research-grade peptides. The site
sells trust: every compound is third-party tested to 99%+ purity, with a
Certificate of Analysis available per batch. Visitors are qualified researchers
and labs deciding whether to buy. The design IS the credibility, so it sits in
the **brand** register (marketing, conversion, identity), not product/tool.

Part of the KJD Capital, LLC family of brands; the visual language is a sibling
to kjdcapital.net (shared Nacelle + Newsreader typography).

## Users

- Qualified researchers, lab purchasers, and independent investigators buying
  reference materials for in vitro / laboratory research only.
- They are technical and skeptical. They want proof (purity, COA, testing),
  clarity (what ships, how fast), and a checkout that feels legitimate and safe.
- Strictly not for human or veterinary use. Every surface must reinforce
  research-use-only framing without feeling like fine-print spam.

## Tone

Confident, precise, clinical-but-warm. Editorial, not salesy. Reads like a
serious materials supplier with exceptional craft, not a supplement brand. Short
declarative sentences. Specifics over adjectives ("99.7% identity purity",
"ships within 24 hours") over vague hype.

## Strategic principles

- Lead with verifiable proof: purity %, third-party testing, COA on request.
- Make the research-use disclaimer present and honest, never buried or sleazy.
- Reduce checkout anxiety: clear totals, real delivery estimates, recognizable
  payment methods, visible security.
- Premium and restrained. White space is a feature. Never look auto-generated.

## Anti-references (do NOT look like these)

- Supplement / nootropic hype stores (neon, "shop now" urgency, stock-photo
  lab coats, countdown timers).
- Generic SaaS landing pages (hero-metric template, identical 3-up icon-card
  grids, gradient-text headlines).
- Dark "biotech crypto" aesthetic (neon-on-black, glowing molecules).
- Anything that reads as AI-generated: centered everything, evenly padded
  cards, em-dash-laden copy, purple-blue gradients.

## Voice rules

- No em dashes. Use commas, colons, periods, or parentheses.
- No restated headings or filler intros. Every word earns its place.
